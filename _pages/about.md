I am a data engineer working on Australian energy market data. I'm the Data Technical Lead at [ZEN Energy](https://www.zenenergy.com.au/), an Australian energy retailer and generator, where I've spent three years building and running the data platform — taking market data from ingestion through to the reporting and forecasting that trading, finance, and executive stakeholders rely on.

Day to day, that means designing and building pipelines end to end on Databricks and Delta Lake, and staying hands-on with the codebase. I'm the in-house subject matter expert for AEMO market data (MMS), MSATS, and PDR, I handled our migration off SQL Server, and I set the pipeline design and data-modelling patterns the rest of the team builds against. I have an active interest in how AEMO market data adds commercial value to the grid — which is what my [NEM Constraint Watch](/projects/nem-constraints/) side project is about.

I came to this from a PhD in distributed systems at the University of Melbourne and more than half a decade lecturing at the University of Moratuwa in Sri Lanka. Before that, I was a Java engineer on the WSO2 Carbon Kernel. The research and the teaching both still pay off: I'm used to translating complex, regulated data into insight for technical teams and senior decision-makers alike, and to tackling a hard problem methodically.

Skills:
* Energy market domain: `AEMO NEM market data (MMS), MSATS, PDR, NEM constraints`
* Languages and data: `Python, SQL, PySpark, Java, C++, Bash, Apache Spark, pandas, NoSQL`
* Platforms and tooling: `Databricks, Delta Lake, Azure Data Factory, Databricks Workflows`
* Automation and AI: `ETL and pipeline automation, AI-assisted development (Claude Code, Databricks Genie)`
* Ways of working: `Data migration and integration, microservices, RESTful APIs, Agile/Scrum, TDD`

Away from the keyboard, I ride bikes — road, gravel, and cross-country — and read science fiction and biographies.
